About Karine

Architecture, painting, radio

Karine Parfenova is a practical architect with more than fifteen years of built work, from intimate interiors to complex urban interventions. Her practice is grounded in precise detailing, generous light, and an empathetic understanding of how people actually live with architecture every day.

As a painter, Karine explores colour, texture, and rhythm as extensions of architectural space. On air, she hosts the radio show Architecture Then And Now, inviting listeners in Luxembourg and beyond into accessible conversations about buildings, cities, and the images that shape them.

Across all three disciplines, her work is guided by a simple idea: building dreams—turning personal stories into spaces, canvases, and dialogues that feel both timeless and distinctly of today.

Painting

Colour as architecture

Karine’s paintings often begin as fragments of façades, street corners, and interior shadows observed in Luxembourg and the cities she travels through. On canvas, these fragments become layered compositions where colour carries the weight of structure.

Architecture Then And Now

Radio show by Karine

Architecture Then And Now is Karine Parfenova’s radio show dedicated to architecture and painting. Broadcast from Luxembourg, it connects historic references with contemporary practice, making design conversations open to everyone—not only architects.

Each episode invites guests and listeners to explore how buildings and images shape daily life: from housing and public space to studios, museums, and the streets in between.

Selected episode themes

  • Luxembourg at Street Level Walking through the city to read façades, thresholds, and everyday details.
  • Studios, Stages, Interiors How artists and musicians shape the rooms they work in.
  • Drawing with Sound Translating architectural spaces into listening experiences on radio.

The show welcomes collaborations with cultural institutions, architecture practices, galleries, and broadcasters in Luxembourg and across Europe.

Approach

From idea to lived space

Human stories first

Every project begins with listening. Karine looks closely at how clients move, work, and rest, translating their stories into precise spatial sequences.

Drawing across mediums

Sketches, models, paintings, and sound all play a role in the design process, ensuring that the final space feels both carefully considered and effortlessly simple.

Building dreams, responsibly

Material choices, daylight strategies, and compact planning aim for sustainable, long-life buildings that remain generous, adaptable, and deeply personal.
